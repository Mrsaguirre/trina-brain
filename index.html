                            🌅 Your ADHD-Friendly Routines
                            Routines that support your brain — not fight it. Built around your natural patterns, not neurotypical standards.
                            ☀️ Morning Routine — 20 Minutes
                            6:30–6:45 AM · Body First
                            Wake up, water, move
                            Drink a full glass of water before anything else. 5-minute walk or stretch. No phone for the first 10 minutes — your ADHD brain needs to regulate before it can plan.
                            6:45–7:00 AM · Brain Prep
                            HALT check + ONE task decision
                            Check: Hungry? Angry? Lonely? Tired? Fix any YES first. Then open Work Notebook and write ONE non-negotiable for the day. That's it. No full planning yet.
                            9:00 AM · Power Session Starts
                            Alarm fires — everything stops
                            When this alarm fires, you sit down, close distractions, open ONE task. No exceptions. This is your dopamine window. Guard it like a client appointment.
                            🌙 Evening Routine — 10 Minutes
                            4:30 PM · Shutdown Ritual
                            The non-negotiable off switch
                            Check off completed tasks. Write tomorrow's ONE task in Work Notebook. Open tomorrow's first file so there's zero friction to start. Say out loud: "I am done for today." Your brain needs an explicit OFF signal.
                            9:00 PM · Brain Download
                            Clear your mental RAM
                            Spend 5 minutes writing anything still floating in your head into your Home Master List. Don't organize it — just dump it. This prevents the 2 AM "I just remembered" spiral.
                            My Keystone Morning Habit
                            Examples: making coffee before opening phone · 5-min walk · writing ONE task first
                            ⚡ Energy Banking — Protect Your Mental Resources
                            ADHD brains don't have 8 equal hours of energy. You have a budget. Spend it wisely or go bankrupt by 2 PM.
                            Your Energy Bank Reality
                            You have approximately 2–3 hours of peak executive function daily. After that, you're running on fumes. Stop trying to be "productive" for 8 hours — it's neurologically impossible for your brain.
                            Task-Energy Matching
                            HIGH ⚡
                            WGI strategy · Proposals · LinkedIn · Client calls · 9–11 AM
                            MED 🔋
                            Email · Research · GHL updates · Templates · 11 AM–2 PM
                            LOW 🪫
                            Filing · Formatting · Briona handoffs · 3–5 PM
                            The HALT Energy Check
                            Before calling yourself lazy or distracted, check:

                            H — Hungry? (eat first)
                            A — Angry/anxious? (5-min walk)
                            L — Lonely? (text someone real)
                            T — Tired? (10-min rest — not scrolling)

                            ADHD brains cannot function through body signals. These aren't excuses — they're prerequisite conditions.
                            Energy Protection Rules
                            ✓ Never schedule high-stakes work after 2 PM
                            ✓ Protect 9–11 AM like a paid client appointment
                            ✓ One context per day (themed days aren't optional)
                            ✓ Lunch is not optional — it's performance fuel
                            ✓ Transitions need 10-min warnings — set phone alerts
                            🔑 Your Keystone Habit
                            The one change that makes everything else easier. For ADHD brains, keystone habits create automatic chain reactions that reduce the number of decisions your executive function has to make.
                            What is a Keystone Habit?
                            A keystone habit is a single behavior that automatically triggers other positive behaviors. For example: "When I make my coffee, I open my Work Notebook" — the coffee becomes a trigger for the notebook, which triggers the Top 3, which triggers the Power Session.

                            Your ADHD brain loves this because it converts multiple decisions into ONE decision.
                            Finding YOUR Keystone
                            Ask: "What is already happening consistently in my day that I could attach a new habit to?"

                            Candidates for Trina:
                            • Making morning coffee → open Work Notebook
                            • Sitting at desk → write ONE task on sticky note
                            • Phone alarm at 9 AM → close all tabs, open ONE task
                            • Evening tea → 5-min brain dump into Home Master List
                            My Work Keystone Habit
                            Write it as a trigger → response. Example: "When my 9AM alarm fires, I close all tabs and open my ONE task."
                            My Home Keystone Habit
                            Example: "When I make coffee, I check my Home Master List for today's priorities."
                            The 30-Day Keystone Rule
                            Pick ONE keystone habit. Do it for 30 days before adding anything else. Your ADHD brain cannot build multiple new habits simultaneously — it overloads executive function and you abandon everything.

                            One habit. Thirty days. Then add the next.
                            🔄 Structured Flexibility
                            The ADHD paradox: you need structure to function AND you rebel against rigid structure. The solution is structured flexibility — firm anchors with flexible space in between.
                            Your 3 Non-Negotiable Anchors
                            These three things are fixed. Everything else floats:

                            🔔 9:00 AM — Power Session starts
                            🔔 12:30 PM — Lunch break (mandatory)
                            🔔 4:30 PM — Work ends

                            Set these as repeating phone alarms right now if they're not already set.
                            The Flex Zone Rules
                            Between anchors, you have full flexibility in HOW you work, but not WHAT you work on. On Content Tuesday, you can work in any order, any location, any tool — as long as it's content work.

                            This gives your ADHD brain the novelty it craves within a structure it can follow.
                            When Plans Fall Apart (Because They Will)
                            ADHD brains don't fail because they have bad days. They fail because one bad day becomes "I've ruined everything" and they abandon the system entirely.

                            The rule: you only need to restart from the next anchor. Missed your 9 AM Power Session? Start fresh at 12:30. Missed that? Start fresh tomorrow morning. The system always has a re-entry point.
                            The "Good Enough" Standard
                            Done and sent is worth 100x more than perfect in drafts.

                            The 80% rule: if something is 80% of where you want it, and the missing 20% won't change the outcome for the person receiving it — send it. Move on. Progress beats perfection in a 6-month sprint.
                            💔 Rejection Sensitivity Dysphoria (RSD)
                            RSD is the ADHD wound that runs everything. It makes criticism feel catastrophic, silence feel like rejection, and success feel temporary. Understanding it changes everything.
                            What RSD Looks Like for Trina
                            • The voice: "everyone else can do this, why can't you?"
                            • Avoiding sending proposals because they might say no
                            • Checking LinkedIn metrics and spiraling if engagement is low
                            • Taking client silence as personal rejection
                            • Interpreting honest feedback as proof of failure
                            • Overworking to preemptively avoid criticism
                            The RSD Reality Check
                            "Is this rejection real, or am I anticipating it?"
                            90% of RSD responses are to imagined rejection. Name it out loud: "This is RSD talking, not reality."
                            Breaking Free from Emotional Hijacking
                            When an emotion takes over, your ADHD brain goes offline — executive function shuts down completely. You can't logic your way out. You have to physically regulate first:

                            1. Name it: "I am feeling ___ right now."
                            2. Locate it: Where in your body do you feel it?
                            3. Breathe: In 4, hold 4, out 6. Twice.
                            4. Ask: "What is ONE small thing I can do right now?"
                            Building Unshakeable Self-Compassion
                            Shame and self-criticism activate the same threat-response as physical danger. A brain in threat mode cannot access executive function — the exact thing ADHD already impairs.

                            Self-compassion isn't soft. It's strategic. Say this:

                            "This is hard. I am struggling. And I am not broken."

                            This is neuroscience, not affirmations.
                            🔬 The Neuroscience of Your ADHD
                            Understanding your brain changes everything. This is not a character flaw. This is a different operating system.
                            Dr. Russell Barkley's Core Insight
                            "ADHD isn't a disorder of not knowing what to do. It's a disorder of not being able to DO what you know."
                            This is why information alone doesn't fix ADHD. You need systems designed for executive dysfunction.
                            Why Your Brain Works This Way
                            ADHD brains have lower baseline dopamine and norepinephrine. This means:

                            • Motivation requires novelty or urgency (not just importance)
                            • Time blindness is neurological — only NOW and NOT NOW exist
                            • Emotions are felt at 2–3× the intensity of neurotypical brains
                            • Task initiation requires more activation energy than others
                            • Hyperfocus is real — and uncontrollable without systems
                            What This Means for Your Systems
                            • Structure must be external (notebooks, alarms, themed days)
                            • Motivation must be engineered (finish-line rewards, gamification)
                            • Time must be made visible (analog clocks, timers, time blocks)
                            • Tasks must be made tiny (2-minute launches, bullet points first)
                            • Emotions must be regulated before executive function can work
